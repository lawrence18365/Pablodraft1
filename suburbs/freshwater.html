Serving Freshwater’s Diverse Community

Freshwater has about 9,186 residents with a median age around 37 and a high median weekly household income (~$2,726). Homes are a mix of apartments (53.1%) and freestanding houses (41.7%), with some semis/townhouses (4.7%). Owner‑occupancy sits near 60.7%; tenure is balanced across owned outright (27.6%), owned with a mortgage (33.1%) and rented (36.6%). English is spoken at home by 84.5%; other common languages include Spanish (1.8%), Portuguese (1.6%), Italian (1.1%), German (1.0%) and French (1.0%).

Freshwater is a classic beach suburb with strong family presence (about 69.9% family households) and an active, outdoors lifestyle shaped by its beach, rockpool and coastal headlands.

Understanding Freshwater’s Unique Cleaning Challenges

Beachfront conditions bring salt air, sand and humidity; headlands and reserves add seasonal pollen and grit. Apartments require low‑moisture methods and efficient access; houses often feature premium natural fibres that benefit from wool‑safe chemistry and residue‑free rinses to maintain softness and appearance.

Our eco‑friendly, soap‑free, wool‑safe multi‑step process delivers deep cleaning without sticky residues, supporting faster dry times and longer‑lasting freshness in both apartment and family‑home settings.

Location & Accessibility

About 17 km north‑east of the Sydney CBD within Northern Beaches Council, Freshwater borders Curl Curl (north), Queenscliff/Manly (south/south‑east), North Manly (west) and Brookvale (north‑west), with the Tasman Sea to the east.

Transport access includes Oliver Street and Harbord Road, close to B‑Line (B1) stops at Manly Vale and Warringah Mall (Brookvale) for frequent services to Wynyard; local routes 166 (Frenchs Forest–Manly via Dee Why Beach) and 167 (Warringah Mall–Manly via South Curl Curl) serve the area.

Protecting High‑Value Properties

Recent medians indicate houses around $3.90m and units about $1.275m. With a majority apartment market and significant freestanding houses, our factory‑grade fabric protection and natural‑fibre expertise help protect furnishings and maintain value across property types.

A Deeper Dive into the Freshwater Community

Covering roughly 1.8 km², Freshwater’s highlights include Freshwater Beach and its 50 m rockpool, the McKillop Park headland and Coast Walk with the ‘Signal Fire’ public artwork, and Freshwater View Reserve.

Local character is enriched by history: known as Freshwater historically, officially renamed Harbord on 1 Sep 1923, and reverted to Freshwater on 12 Jan 2008; surfing heritage includes Duke Kahanamoku’s demonstrations on 24 Dec 1914 at Freshwater and Jan 1915 at Dee Why, and a Surf Life Saving Club founded in 1908.

Attractions include Harbord Diggers (88 Evans St), the Duke Kahanamoku statue and park, and coastal viewpoints along McKillop Park and Freshwater View Reserve. Family‑friendly amenities include the Freshwater Beach Accessible Playground (opened Nov 2024), Jacka Park’s upgraded playground (Dec 2022), and schools such as Harbord Public (K–6), St John the Baptist Catholic Primary (K–6) and NBSC Freshwater Senior Campus (Years 11–12 nearby Curl Curl). We support this vibrant beach community with eco‑friendly upholstery cleaning and professional carpet care across the Northern Beaches.
