Serving Frenchs Forest’s Diverse Community

Frenchs Forest has about 14,267 residents and very high owner‑occupancy (~83.1%). Detached family homes dominate (92.5% separate houses), with limited semis/townhouses (4.3%) and a small apartment share (3.1%). Tenure is weighted to owned with a mortgage (46.3%) and owned outright (36.8%), with a smaller rented segment (15.7%). Median weekly household income is around $3,109. English is spoken at home by 80.5%; other common languages include Mandarin (2.7%), Armenian (1.6%), French (1.4%), Cantonese (1.3%) and Italian (0.9%).

It’s a high‑income, family‑heavy suburb (86.3% family households) with large homes (60.5% with 4+ bedrooms) and high car ownership (71.6% with 2+ vehicles). Named after 19th‑century landholder James French, the area was rural through the early–mid 20th century. Major rezoning in the 2010s delivered the Northern Beaches Hospital, town centre works and significant road upgrades.

Bushland edges around Garigal National Park and Manly Dam bring seasonal dust, pollen and leaf litter, while family activity levels and larger floor areas drive higher traffic patterns. Many homes feature premium natural fibres and valuable upholstery that benefit from careful, wool‑safe processes.

Our eco‑friendly, soap‑free, wool‑safe multi‑step cleaning removes embedded soils without sticky residues, supports faster dry times, and protects fibres — ideal for high‑use family rooms, stairs and bedrooms in larger homes.

Location & Accessibility

Roughly 20 km from the Sydney CBD within Northern Beaches Council, Frenchs Forest borders Belrose (north/north‑west), Oxford Falls (north‑east), Beacon Hill (east), Allambie Heights (south‑east), Seaforth (south), Forestville (south‑west) and Davidson (west).

Key arterials include A38 Warringah Road, Forest Way and Wakehurst Parkway. The Warringah Road underpass (opened March 2020) eased east‑west flow near the hospital precinct. Bus routes along Frenchs Forest Road (136/141/169/193/280) connect to Dee Why, Warringah Mall, Manly and Chatswood, with direct CBD services via Wynyard.

Protecting High‑Value Properties

Recent medians indicate houses around $2.35m and units near $1.509m (unit figures reflect low local stock and sales). With detached homes dominant, our factory‑grade fabric protection and natural‑fibre expertise help preserve furnishings and carpets across multi‑living zones and open‑plan layouts.

A Deeper Dive into the Frenchs Forest Community

Spanning about 9.1 km², local highlights include Manly Dam’s trails and picnic areas, Garigal National Park bushwalks (Bluff and Natural Bridge tracks), Lionel Watts Oval and playground, Warringah Aquatic Centre and Forestway Shopping Centre.

New parkland at Brick Pit Reserve supports the town centre’s growth with boardwalks and playspace. Families benefit from large playgrounds, sports fields and schools including Frenchs Forest Public, Mimosa Public, Arranounbai School and The Forest High School.
